Argumentos Jurídicos — Test Didymi (3 Etapas)
1
Garantía Contractual de Velocidad
El CP BTB Gloria Confidence (cláusula 16) garantiza "about 12.00 knots on a consumption of about 28.0 MT IFO 380 in good weather conditions (BF≤4, SWH≤1.25m)". La garantía es absoluta sujeta únicamente a las exclusiones expresas.
CP Cl.16 garantía Velocidad: 12.00 kts Consumo: 28.0 MT IFO ABT ±0.5 kts → floor 11.50
2
Identificación de Período de Buena Meteorología
32.5 horas consecutivas (15:00 09-Feb → 23:30 10-Feb) con BF≤3 y SWH≤0.9m. Bajo The Ocean Virgo [2015], 14–16 hrs es muestra suficiente. Nuestros 32.5 hrs superan ampliamente el umbral. El período ocurrió en el Mar Amarillo al inicio del voyage.
32.5 hrs BT consecutivas BF máx 3, SWH máx 0.9m The Ocean Virgo [2015] EWHC 3405
3
Velocidad Observada en Buena Meteorología
Durante el período GW, la velocidad observada fue 10.76 kts (promedio ponderado de 3 noon reports en periodo GW). Esto es inferior al ABT floor de 11.50 kts. La brecha confirma incumplimiento prima facie. No se alega corriente favorable que pudiera explicar la deficiencia.
10.76 kts observado Brecha: −1.24 kts vs CP ABT floor 11.50 → incumplimiento The Didymi [1988] 2 LLR 108
4
Extrapolación al Voyage Completo
Conforme a The Gas Enterprise [1993] 2 LLR 352, el rendimiento en buena meteorología se extrapola a todo el viaje. El tiempo perdido se calcula mar de boya a mar de boya: 10,787 NM ÷ 10.76 kts = 1,002.3 hrs observadas vs 10,787 ÷ 12.00 kts = 899.0 hrs teóricas. Pérdida: 103.3 horas = USD 62,350.

Metodología ABT — Por Qué USD 62,350 y No USD 38,860
El ABT (±0.5 kts) tiene UNA ÚNICA función: determinar si existe incumplimiento (floor 11.50 kts). Una vez confirmado el incumplimiento, el quantum se calcula SIEMPRE contra la velocidad garantizada del CP (12.00 kts). Calcular contra el ABT floor (11.50 kts) sería un double-counting que beneficiaría incorrectamente al armador.

Riesgo: Argumento de Corriente Favorable
El armador podría alegar que la corriente del Pacífico Norte (Kuroshio) explica parte de la deficiencia. Sin embargo: (1) Las velocidades GW fueron medidas en el Mar Amarillo, no en el Pacífico. (2) Los noon reports de la travesía transpacífica también muestran deficiencia consistente. (3) La distribución bimodal de velocidades (Feb alto vs Mar bajo) apunta a degradación mecánica, no factores externos.
Riesgo: Discrepancia en Datos Meteorológicos
El armador podría impugnar la clasificación GW de 3 reportes marcados como "⚑ Potential". Recomendación: Obtener datos ECMWF/Copernicus para corroborar los datos del Capitán. En cualquier caso, los 2 reportes GW indiscutidos (09F, 10F) por sí solos muestran 10.76 kts vs 12.00 kts.

Owners / Disponent Owners MV Yangtze Brightness (IMO 9953365) CP: BTB Gloria Confidence dated 14 February 2025 Dear Sirs, RE: SPEED CLAIM — VOYAGE PATACHE TO JINZHOU CP CLAUSE 16 — UNDERPERFORMANCE IN GOOD WEATHER CONDITIONS CLAIM: USD 62,350 We write on behalf of Charterers, BTB Transport S.A., in connection with the above voyage. 1. FACTUAL BACKGROUND MV Yangtze Brightness (IMO 9953365) performed the voyage from Puerto Patache, Chile to Jinzhou, China under the terms of the BTB Gloria Confidence Charter Party dated 14 February 2025. The vessel departed Patache on 9 February 2025 at 15:00 LT and arrived at Jinzhou anchorage on 3 April 2025 at 04:00 LT. The total sea distance was 10,787 nautical miles. 2. CONTRACTUAL WARRANTIES (CP CLAUSE 16) The Charter Party warrants vessel speed of "about 12.00 knots on a consumption of about 28.0 MT IFO 380" in good weather conditions, defined as Beaufort Scale ≤4 and Significant Wave Height ≤1.25 metres. The "about" allowance (ABT) is ±0.5 knots, establishing a minimum warranted speed floor of 11.50 knots for the purpose of determining breach only. 3. GOOD WEATHER PERFORMANCE Applying the methodology established in The Didymi [1988] 2 Lloyd's Rep 108 and The Ocean Virgo [2015] EWHC 3405: Good weather period identified: 09 Feb 15:00 — 10 Feb 23:30 (32.5 consecutive hours) Location: Yellow Sea / approaches to Jinzhou Conditions: BF ≤3, SWH ≤0.9m throughout Observed speed (GW average): 10.76 knots This sample of 32.5 hours substantially exceeds the 14–16 hour threshold confirmed in The Ocean Virgo [2015] as sufficient for extrapolation purposes. 4. BREACH OF WARRANTY The observed good weather speed of 10.76 knots falls below the ABT floor of 11.50 knots, establishing breach of the speed warranty under CP Clause 16. 5. QUANTUM — METHODOLOGY (ABT CORRECTLY APPLIED) We emphasise that the ABT tolerance has ONE function only: determining whether a breach exists. Once breach is confirmed, quantum is calculated against the FULL warranted speed (12.00 knots), not against the ABT floor. Calculating quantum against the ABT floor (11.50 knots) would constitute impermissible double-counting of the ABT allowance. Time actually taken: 10,787 NM ÷ 10.76 kts = 1,002.3 hours Time warranted: 10,787 NM ÷ 12.00 kts = 899.0 hours Time lost: 103.3 hours Hire rate: USD 19,167 per day = USD 798.63 per hour Claim: 103.3 hrs × USD 798.63 = USD 62,350 (For reference only: the incorrect calculation against the ABT floor would yield USD 38,860, understating the true loss by USD 23,490.) 6. EXTRAPOLATION PRINCIPLE Pursuant to The Gas Enterprise [1993] 2 Lloyd's Rep 352, the underperformance identified in good weather conditions is extrapolated across the full voyage from sea buoy to sea buoy. The owners have not identified any mechanical defect or exceptional circumstances that would explain or excuse the underperformance. 7. Interest under section 49 of the Arbitration Act 1996 will be claimed from the date of this letter at a commercial rate. We look forward to your prompt response.
